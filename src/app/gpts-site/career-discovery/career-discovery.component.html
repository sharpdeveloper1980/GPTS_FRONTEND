Work Styles in Career discovery

Each of the 8 Work Styles was built as a combination of your score amongst 24 sub-factors (strengths) of the Thriving Index.

Strengths in this scenario relate to personally rewarding patterns of emotions and behaviours that can be productively applied. Career Discovery Tool has the unique ability to focus on your abilities and direct you towards practical careers that can use your talents. It can guide you towards a career that you are best suited for and help hone your remarkable skills to perfection.

Assessment parameters

The world’s first and only strengths-based career assessment

ANALYTICAL

Those with an Analytical tendency work better in roles which require problem-solving, analysis and interpretation of figures or data.

Analytical

SPECIALIST

Specialist roles often involve building specialism, problem-solving and completing tasks with accuracy and precision.

Specialist

COMMUNITY

Community roles often involve working towards a greater goal, require high levels of empathy, cooperation, resilience and an internal drive to help others.

Community

CREATIVE

Those with a Dominant creative style are often curious, innovative, flexible individuals who work through various mediums (artistic, visual, written) to represent and express their ideas.

Creative

ENTERPRISING

Those with an Enterprising outlook tend to identify needs, opportunities and have the confidence to take actions and perservere through challenges.

Enterprising

RELATIONAL

Individuals with a Relational workstyle tend to work better with and around people and value teamwork and cooperation.

Relational

PERSUASIVE

Persuasive individuals work better in customer-facing roles that require confidence, negotiation and diplomacy.

Persuasive

PRACTICAL

Those who tend to have a Practical style are usually more hands-on, task-oriented and highly organised.

Practical
